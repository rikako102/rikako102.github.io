I am the co-founder and co-director of MakerGirl, Northwestern academy.

[January 2018 - present]


Founded in 2014 at University of Illinois Urbana Champaign, MakerGirl is a non-profit startup that aims to educate young girls about STEM and inspire passion for innovation and design.   At each themed session, girls learn about 3D printing and then design and print their own 3D object to take home.

With the help of my co-founders, Nancy Yao and Amanda Mirande, we began meeting with the UIUC team and organizing our own Northwestern Academy.

We've done a lot for MakerGirl! Like, we...

  • Built a network from scratch of Evanston and surrounding Chicago area elementary schools, public libraries, and parent associations
  • Organized four successful sessions with full sign-ups of 8-12 girls
  • Laid the foundation of the Northwestern Academy, establishing session logistics and building a team of 12 university students
  • Partnered with established organizations, such as the Girl Scouts of Greater Chicago and Northwest Indiana and Codeverse, and businesses such as Fast Radius

The girls do all the designing and CAD modeling on their own... with a little help, of course.



We have bi-monthly sessions of up to 12 girls, each with a unique theme to focus their brainstorming. The girls create their design on paper and transfer it onto TinkerCad, a drag-and-drop computer modeling website. The main focus of this portion is problem solving and innovation — what is the best way to make their drawing come to life with simple 3D shapes like cubes, cylinders, and pyramids?






The girls watch their prints come to life in the printers...








... and get to take them home as a reminder of what they're capable of!
