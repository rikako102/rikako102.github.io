OXO

[June - September 2017]

I was a Product Development Intern for OXO, an innovative consumer products company. I specifically worked with the Cleaning and Organization team, who dealt with products like household cleaning supplies, bathroom storage, and kitchen organization.

OXO is a titan in the industry for their incredible products that are centered around, first and foremost, the user. A wall in their offices is covered floor to ceiling with lost gloves of all kinds - big or small, child or adult, fashion or utility - to remind the employees that we're working to create for everyone, regardless of age, gender, or ability. With this internship, I contributed to OXO's thoughtful process as a problem becomes an idea becomes a product.

My main task of the summer: begin the development process for a future cleaning product (not yet released). By the end of my ten weeks, I completed most of the user research and benchmark testing. This started with diving deep into competitive products, identifying white spaces in the market that our product could fill, and figuring out what consumers really thought about existing products — good and bad. We test to inform our final product decisions with real user experiences.
With these notes in mind, the next step was creating a more knowledgable base and seeing first-hand the major problems users run into with the best products on the market. ran several rounds of testing. For all, I made a concoction designed to mimic something hard to clean. The first round was for myself and my manager. We applied the mixture to several surfaces and tested benchmark products and functional prototypes to immerse ourselves in the experience and become experts of this cleaning tool. The final segment of testing was in-person tests. We identified several people outside of the office who often use the cleaning tool in question and visited them to see their process. We were able to see the quirks that arise in normal routines that words alone aren't enough to descibe usually thought of when asked to describe a process.
