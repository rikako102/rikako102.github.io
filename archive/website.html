I coded this website from scratch, with help from the Internet and friends.

[May 2018 - present]

I've been writing my name for decades and I still had trouble making it look nice.


"How hard could it be to build my own website from scratch?", I thought.

Turns out, pretty hard.
This labor of love has been in constant flux ever since I bought the domain name. I set out to build a website that spoke directly to my creativity and skillsets while being a pleasant experience for those visiting to see my work.

This entire website is a passion project and a visual representation of all the skills I picked up along the way. Like,
  • HTML, CSS (& animation), JavaScript — to create the bones, looks, and actions of each page and interaction
  • Procreate — to create all the hand-drawn icons and titles (yes, those scribbles are all me!)
  • Adobe Photoshop — to edit and enhance my images
  • Concise Storytelling — to tell you my story without drowning you in words
  • Graphic Design — to visually communicate who I am through this website


There are 3 core design decisions I made early on.

This website will be friendly and personal.

You're meeting me. I want it to feel like that. The portfolios I'm most drawn to are the ones that make me smile.

This website will be minimal.

You shouldn't have to dig around to find what you're looking for. Navigation is intuitive.

This website will be beautiful.

There will be features that have no use other than for aesthetic's sake, to breathe personality into the website.

This website is a constant work in progress...










...and will no doubt look entirely different in a few months.

As with any good product development, any user comments or feedback is extremely valuable to me. Tell me what you really think!
